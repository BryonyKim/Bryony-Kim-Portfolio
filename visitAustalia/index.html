Watersports

Australia offers an array of watersports including surfing, snorkelling and diving as well as boat trips for the less energetic. Head anywhere on Australia's extensive coastline.

Scenery

Australia offers scenery beyond beaches and coastline, just head inland to experience the wonders of Uluru and Kata Tjuta as well as mountains, gorges and termite mounds.

Wildlife

Australia has an assortment of native animals from the large land roaming kangaroo to the huge salt water crocodile found in the Northern Regions of this incredible country.

Culture

Australia has some interesting tales to tell, whether you are heading deep into the bush, exploring aboriginal territory or visiting opal mining towns with underground houses.

Ningaloo Reef

Western Australia

Don't miss Ningaloo Reef on Australia's West Coast. It offers beautiful, colourful coral as well as an assortment of under-water species to keep your senses alive.

Wildlife

Of course, keep your eyes open for native wildlife

Whichever state, there is native wildlife to spot, either on land or underwater.

Tasmania

Don't miss Tassie!

The beauty Tasmania offers makes the 10hr ferry trip well worth it. Beaches, walking tracks and caves, Tasmania has it all.

Don't miss!

Whales

Watch migrating whales from shore or boat.

Migrating whales can be seen from both the East and West coast. Head to Exmouth, Western Australia, for a once in a lifetime whale watching boat trip.

Coober Pedy

South Australia

South Australia's Coober Pedy is well worth a visit and not just for the opals! The residents there live underground and there are many underground activities for tourists.

Whitsundays

White sand and crystal clear water

Head to the Whitsundays, Queensland, for a picture perfect view.
